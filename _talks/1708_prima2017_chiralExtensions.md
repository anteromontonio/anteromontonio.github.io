**Abstract**: Abstract polytopes are objects that generalize convex polytopes on a combinatorial way. An elementary problem in convex polytopes is, given an n-polytope K, is there an (n + 1)-polytope P such that all the facets of P are isomorphic to K? When this problem is presented in the context of abstract polytopes it becomes interesting when symmetry conditions are imposed over P (and hence, over K). In the talk we will explain the problem with detail when both P and K are chiral polytopes. In particular we will give some results regarding the possibilities for the Schl&auml;fli type of P and some examples for small values of n.
