**Abstract**: Convex polyhedra and tilings of the Euclidean of hyperbolic plane can be thought as a family of polygons glued together along their edges. This idea extends to higher dimensions: a 4-dimensional cube can be thought as family of 3-dimensional cubes glued together along its 2-dimensional faces. This way of thinking polytopes gives rise to some natural questions: given a n-dimensional polytope K, can we build a (n+1)-dimensional polytope P such that all the facets (maximal proper faces of P) are isomorphic to K? how many of such P can we build? Usually the geometry plays an important role on those questions.In the talk we will explore variants of this question in the context of abstract polytopes, where the geometry is less relevant. In particular, we are interested in the situation where prescribed symmetry conditions are imposed on P.
