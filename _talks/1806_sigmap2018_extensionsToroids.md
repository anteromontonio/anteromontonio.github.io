**Abstract**: An abstract polytope $\mathcal{K}$ is an extension of an abstract polytope $\mathcal{P}$ if all the facets of $\mathcal{K}$ are isomorphic to $\mathcal{P}$. Chiral polytopes are those that have all possible rotations but lack of reflectional symmetry. In the talk we will explore the problem of building chiral extensions of polytopes. We will also show a technique to build chiral extensions of regular toroids.
